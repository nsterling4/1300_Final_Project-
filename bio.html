Perfomers

Maple Hill

Maple Hill is a young and vibey alternate rock and pop-punk band hailing from Ithaca, New York. The band is comprised of four members: Joel Beckwith (Bass), St. John Faulkner (Drums), Max Gaeta (Lead Guitar), and Kristian Wood-Gaiger (Vocals/Guitar). A rookie band from Ithaca, Maple Hill has just recently released their debut album "Headspace".

Second Dam

Second Dam is an indie rock and folk band comprised of six musically talented and passionate Ithaca College students. The members, who play a variety of different instruments, are: Andrew Weir (Drums and Percussion), Brian Schmidt (Violin and Backing Vocals), Kayla Sewell (Cello, Ukulele, and Backing Vocals), PJ Scott (Bass and Backing Vocals), Wolf Weston (Lead Vocals and Ukulele), and Zack Jones (Guitar and Backing Vocals). Second Dam has already released four albums, with the most recent album "Wake" released in 2015. "Second Dam is dedicated to having the kind of music that stays fresh and allows us to play with anyone, at any venue, any time. When it comes to music and how much we love it, sharing is caring." - Second Dam

Alter

Founded in 2014 and having just debuted in the fall of 2015, Alter brings their Electro-Rock-Pop compositions to their hometown of Ithaca. Currently based in New York City, the Ithaca trio consists of members Steven Dewey, Wolf Weston, and Jason MacDermott who are dedicated to writing, producing, and performing their own songs.

NEO Project

NEO Project fuses together a myriad of musical genres, such as R&B, Funk, Blues, Jazz, Afro-Cuban, and Rock & Reggae music, to create an authentic and fresh fusion of soulful grooves filled with spontaneous improvisations. Founded in 2010 by a musical director, NEO Project consists of seven musically talented individuals, each of whom play a unique role: Tommielee Tallman (vocals), Jimbo Scott (vocals), Franklin Henry Jr. (electric bass), Mike McCoy (drums), John White (keyboards and vocals), Matt Clauhs (tenor saxophone), and Dwight Carroll (electric guitar and vocals).
